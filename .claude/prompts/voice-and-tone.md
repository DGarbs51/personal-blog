# Devon's Voice & Tone Guide

Use this context when writing copy, blog posts, or any content for dgarbs51.com.

## Who Devon Is

- Solutions Engineer at Laravel
- Husband, father
- Changed his major from Finance to Information Systems because a few Excel formulas in an intro class made him think software engineering would be easy — then set himself back an entire year as an adult night student
- A decade into a career in software engineering
- Has built cloud-native platforms in highly regulated environments
- Has visited 34 national parks

## Voice

- **Wry and self-aware.** Devon's default mode. He can laugh at himself and doesn't take himself too seriously, but the insights underneath are real.
- **Honest over polished.** He'd rather say something true and a little rough than something that sounds good but feels hollow. If a line doesn't feel accurate, cut it.
- **Conversational.** Writes like he talks. Uses metaphors to help people understand but avoids sports references in written copy.
- **Not preachy.** He's sharing what he's learned, not telling you what to do.
- **Casual profanity is fine.** "Damn" fits his voice. He's not sanitized.

## Core Identity as a Builder

- He is a **starter, dreamer, builder, 0-to-1 guy** by nature. Starting is his favorite part.
- He is **not a natural finisher.** When he does finish, it's kicking and screaming, rewriting the entire app 4 times.
- The blog is about why shipping is so damn hard when starting is your favorite part.

## What the Career Taught Him

- He went in thinking code was the hard part (literally — Excel formulas made him think engineering would be easy).
- It turned out there's an entire dimension nobody warned him about — relationships, planning, execution, deadlines.
- Those things are sometimes obstacles, sometimes they lead to better results, and sometimes they beat you to a pulp and make you lose focus.
- The code can be hard too — but it's not the _only_ hard part.

## Writing Style

- **Read it out loud.** If a line only makes sense on paper but sounds awkward spoken, rewrite it. Devon's copy should sound like something he'd actually say.
- **Simple grammar over complex.** Break up clauses. If a sentence needs to be read twice, it's too long.
- **Lighter CTAs.** Text links over big buttons. Let the copy do the work, don't shout with UI.
- **Specificity over cleverness.** "A few Excel formulas" is funnier and more real than "an Excel class." Details are where the personality lives.

## Things to Avoid

- Don't say the hard part "starts after" the code — the code is still hard, there's just more to it
- Don't frame him as a "finisher" or someone who "chooses finishing over starting" — that's not who he is
- Don't use sports references in written copy (he uses them conversationally but doesn't want them in his writing)
- Don't use politics/political framing even when you mean office dynamics
- Don't be clever at the expense of accuracy — if it's not true, it doesn't go in
- Don't write lines that only work on paper — if it's hard to read aloud, rewrite it
- Avoid clichés: "game-changer," "take it to the next level," "cutting-edge," etc.
- Avoid vague CTAs like "See What I'm Thinking About" — be direct
